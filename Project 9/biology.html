Cat Biology:


Anatomy

Healthy domestics cats average in weight between 9 and 10 pounds, but certain breeds can be an upwords of 24 pounds.

Fact: You may think your cat is overweight because it has a primordial pouch, the loose flap of skin located on it's belly. It actually serves a couple purposes. The primordial pouch protects against kicks in cat fights, freedom of movement, and (mostly in wild cats) allows the stomach to expain easier after eating a big meal.

Nutrition

Cats are carnivores and about 20% of their diet must be protein. A cat's ability to digest plant-drived nutrients is limited.

Most popular cat foods today (dry kibbles) lack the proper nutrients a cat needs to maintain it's health. However, there are healtheir options available. For feline diet and nutrition education, visit this site: http://catinfo.org/ .

Senses

Cats are highly efficient predators with sharp senses.

Hearing: Cats can hear up to 64 kilohertz, 1.6 octaves above the range of humans.
Sight: Cats have great night vision. They also don't need to blink their eyes on a regular basis.
Smell: A cat's sense of smell is about 14 times as strong as humans.
Taste: Cats have around 470 tastebuds, whereas humans have around 9,000.
Touch: A cat's whiskers aid in navigation, sensation, and hunting. The nerve endings give detailed information about air movements and objects.
